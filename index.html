INSURANCE PHILOSOPHY

To identify and eliminate or reduce as far as practicable the conditions and practices which cause losses, especially as certain categories of claims are not insurable, ie. INSURANCE COMPANIES DEEM RISKS TO BE TRADE RISKS.

When risk cannot be eliminated or reduced to workable levels.

  1. To purchase insurance that will provide indemnity for catastrophe losses.

  2. To retain those risks not considered to be of major importance to the operating position or financial stability of the company.

To retain whatever portion of the risk for the client's account, provided economically viable.

To maximise the overall insurance purchasing power of the company.

To develop throughout the company a positive attitude towards identifying and controlling loss and minimising risk exposures through the implementation of Risk Management techniques.

To consider capital expenditure on risk improvement recommendations, but only where it is cost effective in the medium and long term to implement such improvements.

To purchase insurance from reputable Insurers and to avoid the use of non-registered Insurers and captive markets.
